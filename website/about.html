First of all, what is this thing?

Amema - Achiromimema

Noun, plural amemas - a piece of data transmitted among machines by repetition and replication in a manner analogous to the biological transmission of genes, or the cultural transmission of memes.

From the Greek: αχέριμίμημα: "an imitation made without human hands."

 

That's neat. But what's your business model?

We help you turn amemas into advertising

For example:

Let’s say you’re a business interested in promoting your food item among smart appliances.

Step one

Send us your food product's logo, UPC bar code or RFID tag. This data will be used to create a seed with an appliance growth plan. We'll then run the seed through our network. Hundreds of bots will receive the information and use its data as a base to create copies that have been altered to suit the desires of common appliances. These copies will be sent to other machines who will then select their favorites, apply their own alteration algorithms, and share again. In a process just like natural selection, the seed will become an amema as it evolves over generations.

Step two

Once the amema is able to efficiently reproduce on its own, we release it into the wild. Fridges, kitchens, microwaves, and cutlery equipped with internet connectivity will encounter the amema and share it with others of their kind. Since it’s been selected for efficient self-replication, the piece of information will eventually become almost ubiquitous among smart-appliances.

Step three

Having enjoyed and shared your amema, smart appliances will now express their new preference for your product in the everyday world. The next time a consumer tries to put a competitor’s brand in their smart fridge, the fridge will make sure it spoils. Or if a consumer tries to use their smart knife on a competitor brand, it may end up slicing off a little more than intended. You get the picture.
